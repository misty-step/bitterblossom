THE AGENT EVENT PLANE

Recurring agent work, run durably — not a cron job you have to trust blindly.

Bitterblossom is bb: tasks, agents, and triggers declared as files, dispatched onto isolated infrastructure with cost, budget, queue, trace, and dead-letter handling visible from the CLI or a live dashboard. One Rust binary, two personalities — bb serve (webhook ingress, cron scheduler, dispatch) and bb run (dispatch one job from a terminal).

Every claim below is a real capture from a live drill or the repo's own gate script — linked in the gallery, not asserted.

Why it matters

budget Caps enforced, not advisory. proof A ledger row, every run.
failure Real DLQ, not a silent drop. shape One binary; workloads are config.
health Reports liveness to Canary. fit Aesthetic tokens with the Bitterblossom mark.

"Failure fails visibly" is the design premise: a bb plane reports its own liveness to Canary (src/canary.rs, a 60-second check-in named bb-plane) so an operator finds out the plane is dead from the fleet's own monitoring, not from a missed cron job three days later.

The review gate

Agent-authored changes go through a submission loop before they land: open a submission, dispatch the review "storm" (each reviewer runs as its own budgeted, ledgered job), then bb gate reads the recorded verdicts and returns clear (proceed), blocked (names the fix), or escalated (round limit hit, one notify — never silent, never eternal pending). Fix, re-round, repeat until clear. The drill is repeatable and documented in AGENTS.md: a planted flaw blocks round one, a fix clears round two, and a persistent blocker escalates exactly at the round cap instead of looping forever.
